*By Serj · July 2026 · 4 min read*

I don't know exactly how many tracks I've made with Suno.

I stopped counting at some point. You make one, then another, then a third. The ones that came out well you remember. The rest you don't think about.

## What chanson actually is

Russian chanson gets called a lot of things. Some people use the word to mean prison ballads. Some use it for anything with a guitar and feelings. In my head it just means songs about life — real situations, real emotions, nothing polished into something unrecognizable.

A street scene. A relationship that ended badly. Something you saw and couldn't explain but couldn't forget either. The kind of thing where the words matter more than whether the drop hits at the right millisecond.

That's the niche I ended up in. Not by planning it. Just because that's what I write when I write.

## I'm not a great writer

I want to be clear about this: I am not a poet. I didn't study literature. I'm not working from some deep mastery of language and form.

I write what's on my mind. What's bothering me. What happened this week, or ten years ago, that I still think about. What naболело — there's a Russian word for it that doesn't translate cleanly, something like "what has been aching." What has been sitting inside you long enough that it needs to come out somewhere.

For a long time the words just stayed in notebooks. Or in my head. Because without music they're just text — and nobody cares about text. You can have something completely true and completely real and it doesn't reach anyone because it's missing the thing that carries it.

Suno changed that. For the first time I could give the words a voice. Give them the sound that matched what they were saying. A song about a hard winter night sounds different from a song about summer and it should — and now it actually does.

The words still come from me. What I actually wanted to say, that's still mine. Suno gives it legs.

## About the people who copy

The tracks that do well get copied.

Someone takes the song, changes three words in the lyrics, adjusts the style a little in Suno, uploads it as their own. Some don't even bother changing much. I've seen basically my track on TikTok with a different name on it.

My reaction: I'm not angry. If someone finds it easier to take something that already works rather than sit down and think of their own — that's their problem, not mine. Their relationship with their own conscience. I can't be inside their head and I don't want to be.

The legal situation doesn't help anyway. The rights to a Suno track belong to Suno, more or less — they sing, they play, they built the model that produces it. My words without that music and performance are nothing. Just text. So even if I wanted to fight it legally, I'd be fighting over something I don't fully own.

And honestly — if they're copying it, the track was good enough to be worth copying. That's a strange kind of compliment. I'll take it.

## Why this niche

I didn't choose chanson as a strategy. I didn't think "this genre has low competition in AI music" — though it does, actually. There are thousands of AI music channels making lo-fi beats and drill and hyperpop. There are very few making songs about life in Russian.

I ended up here because this is what I have to say. The genre fits the material. Songs about life — about real things, not fantasies — don't need a perfect mix or cutting-edge production. They need to feel true.

AI-generated music gets criticized for being emotionless, formulaic, all surface. And a lot of it is. But that's not a property of the tool — it's a property of what you feed into it. If you feed it something real, something that came from actual experience, the result carries that.

At least sometimes. Not always. But enough times that I keep going.

## What naболело is enough

I'm not trying to become a famous artist. I'm not trying to build a label or a brand. I make tracks because I have things I need to say and now there's a way to say them that reaches people.

Some of those people copy the tracks. Some share them. Some just listen once and forget. That's fine. That's what music does — it travels and you lose track of it and that's the point.

I'm not a great writer. I just have things on my mind. Turns out that's enough.
