You made a track in Suno. It sounds good in the Suno player. You export it — and something feels off on Spotify or YouTube.

This guide explains exactly why that happens and how to fix it online, for free, without installing anything.

## Why Suno Tracks Need Mastering

Suno optimizes its output for its own preview player. That player is tuned to Suno's export level — typically -10 to -12 LUFS, with peaks close to 0 dBFS.

Streaming platforms work differently. Spotify normalizes to -14 LUFS. Apple Music normalizes to -16 LUFS. When your Suno track arrives louder than the target, the platform turns it down automatically.

The problem: turning down a hot track doesn't remove clipping. If peaks were hitting 0 dBFS in the original, those samples are already distorted. Reducing volume just makes the distortion quieter — it stays audible on good speakers or headphones.

The fix: master before upload.

## What Mastering Does for a Suno Track

A properly mastered Suno track goes through three corrections:

**Loudness normalization** — brings integrated LUFS to the platform target. Spotify gets -14 LUFS. Apple Music gets -16 LUFS. The track plays at the right level without the platform correcting it.

**True peak limiting** — catches peaks that exceed -1 dBTP. This is what removes clipping before it reaches the platform. True peak limiting is different from regular peak limiting — it catches distortion that hides between samples, where most Suno clipping actually occurs.

**High-pass filtering** — removes low-frequency content below 30 Hz. Suno sometimes generates sub-bass rumble in this range that's inaudible but affects how compression and limiting behave. Removing it first produces a cleaner result.

## Quick vs Auto-Master for Suno Tracks

**Quick mode** works well for most Suno tracks. It applies the Wave Clean preset — high-pass filter at 30 Hz, true peak limiter at -1 dBTP, loudness normalization to target. One click, clean result.

**Auto-Master** is worth using when the Analyze tab shows elevated Mud or Harsh readings. It detects your genre automatically — Electronic is the default for most Suno output — and applies genre-appropriate EQ corrections before limiting and normalizing.

If your Suno track has vocals, try Pop or Hip-Hop genre setting instead of Electronic. The EQ curves are optimized differently.

## What to Check Before Uploading

After mastering, verify three things:

- Integrated LUFS is at or near your platform target
- True Peak is at -1 dBTP or below
- The track sounds natural at normal listening volume — not squashed or pumped

If the track sounds over-compressed, the Suno export may have already been heavily limited internally. In that case use Subtle strength in Auto-Master, or reduce limiting in Manual mode.

## The Short Version

Suno exports at -10 to -12 LUFS with clipping peaks. Streaming platforms want -14 to -16 LUFS with true peaks below -1 dBTP. Master the gap before upload — your track will sound noticeably cleaner on every platform.
